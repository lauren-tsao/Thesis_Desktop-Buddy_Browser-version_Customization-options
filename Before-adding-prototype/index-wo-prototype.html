About

MiniKin is a virtual pet that resides on the user’s desktop as an ambient presence.

It supports daily routines through wellness checks, event notifications and weather updates by using implicit behavioral cues rather than explicit notifications.

Concept

MiniKin's Purpose

As a research artifact of my thesis, MiniKin's purpose is to highlight the untapped potential of virtual pets as the face of implicit interaction, pointing towards a new paradigm of UI design.

Themes it explores

  • Virtual pets, like real-life pets, establish emotional connection through behavioral consistency, presence, and responsiveness.

  • Virtual pets can leverage emotional connection to be more effective than conventional interfaces in improving the lives of users through implicit interaction.

Implicit Interaction vs. Explicit Interaction

  • Operates in the background, requiring little to no direct attention from the user.

  • Intuitive and unobtrusive, unlike explicit interactions that are directive and attention-demanding.

  • Proactive, anticipating user needs and acting without requiring direct commands.

Features

Weather Updates

  • Through outfits, MiniKin informs user of the weather (e.g. wears a raincoat when it is raining)

  • Clothing is a form of universal visual language

  • User infers what this means instead of being told that it is raining

Event Notifications

  • On special events, MiniKin will wear an accessory related to the event

  • For example, if it is someone’s birthday, MiniKin will wear a party hat

  • User can then ask MiniKin whose birthday is it

Wellness Checks

  • MiniKin drinks water every 15 mins and stretches every 30 mins

  • A silent encouragement for the user to mirror its actions

  • A behavioral suggestion instead of verbal instruction

Bedtime Reminders

  • MiniKin dozes off at 11.30pm and sleeps until 7:00am

  • A quiet indication for the user to go to bed
